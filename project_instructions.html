Step 3 - Asking questions & Question Categories

Now for the interesting part!

After uploading your desired documents, you should select a category for the question on the right-hand side of the page. This category can be A, B, or C.

Please refer to the following matrix regularly throughout your submissions to ensure you stay aligned with them.

Questions you ask should be about facts, numbers, or information that is readily available in the reports you uploaded. Please do not ask questions that require the model to make assumptions or predictions about the future, or to provide opinions or subjective analysis. The questions should be clear, concise, and specific to the information contained in the reports.

Please don't limit yourself to just asking about numbers. We also encourage you to ask about lawsuits, shareholders and whatever else you can think of that is contained in your uploaded reports.

Step 4 - Providing answers to these questions

After you ask your questions, you should then provide answers to each of the questions you asked. This answer will be used by raters as a reference to ensure the modelâ€™s answers contain the key information youâ€™re looking for. Therefore, to make the rater's lives easier, your answers should be concise and structured. They should have the following structure, depending on how many questions you asked. Depending on the category you selected, the answers will be dynamically structured as follows:

Category A

For Category A, since you are asking a single, simple question, your answer should be concise and directly address the question. Use the following structure:

  • Document:
    • 10-K: <company name> 10-K <year>
      • Example: Amazon 10-K 2024
    • 10-Q: <company name> 10-Q <quarter> <year>
      • Example: Nvidia 10-Q Q2 2024
  • Page: Single number of the page where the answer is found.
    • Example: 23
  • Final Answer: This needs to be straight to the point (one short sentence). Think of the most concise way to present the expected answer.
    • Example:
      • Question: Why did Epic Games file a lawsuit against apple?
      • Final Answer: They alleged violations of federal and antitrust laws based on how they operate their App Store.
